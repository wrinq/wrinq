What does "A small transaction fee" mean?

Akshat Jiwan Sharma

So you have read about wrinq and found that you can probably use it to manage your rent. There is just one tiny problem. You don't know what it costs. The phrase "a small transaction" fee does not inspire much confidence in you. I can probably guess what you are thinking right now.

"A small transaction fee eh? A good way to say I will charge you as much as I want"

I don't blame you. But when I say "a small transaction fee" I really mean it.

"OK so exactly how small is it?"

I am glad you asked. First of all the transaction fee of wrinq is in the same currency in which you collect rent.

So for instance if you charge a rent of $1013 from your tenant you get to take $992.74 while wrinq gets $20.26.

If you charge RS 1013 you get to take RS 992.74 while wrinq gets RS 20.26.

I hope you get the picture.

"But my rent is not $1013"
